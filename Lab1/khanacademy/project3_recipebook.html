Recipe #1: Ratatouille

  • Time: 1 hr 30 mins
  • Serves: 1 portion
Ingredients Quantity
Tomato paste 1 can
Onion 1/2
Minced garlic 1/4
Water 3/4 cup
Olive oil 4 tablespoons
Small eggplant 1 can
Zucchini 1
Yellow squash 1
Red bell pepper 1
Yellow bell pepper 1

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
Step 2: Spread tomato paste onto the bottom of a 10-inch square baking dish. Sprinkle with onion and garlic. Stir in water and 1 tablespoon olive oil until throughly combined. Season with salt and pepper.
Step 3: Layer alternating slices of eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red and yellow bell peppers concentrically from the edge to the center, slightly overlapping for color.
Step 4: Drizzle vegetables with 3 tablespoons olive oil, season with salt, pepper, and thyme. Cover with parchment paper cut to fit.
Step 5: Bake in the preheated oven until vegetables are roasted and tender, about 45 mintes. Serve with dollops of mascarpone cheese.

Recipe #2: Baozi

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 24 bao
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 5 cups (600g)
Active dry yeast 2 tsp
Sugar 2 tsp
Water 1 1/4 cup
Napa cabbage 1 pound
Rice 1/4 cup (30g)
Dark soy sauce 1 1/4 tbsp
Light soy sauce 1 1/4 tbsp
Salt 1/4 tbsp
Sugar 1/4 tbsp

Step 1: Make the dough

  1. Add flour, yeast, sugar, and oil to a medium mixing bowl and stir to combine.
  2. Gradually add water while stirring with a fork until a soft, smooth dough forms. Press with your hands if needed. Alternatively, use a stand mixer with a dough hook on slow speed.
  3. Knead for 5 – 10 minutes until dough is smooth without any lumps
  4. Place dough in mixing bowl and cover with a damp towel. Let rise for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until doubled in size.
Step 2: Make the filling
  1. While the dough is rising, make your filling. Use clean hands to squeeze out any excess water from your napa cabbage. Combine the pork, cabbage, ginger, rice cooking wine, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, salt, and sugar in a mixing bowl. Stir until well-combined.
Step 3: Fill and wrap your bao
  1. After your dough has doubled in size, gently punch down with your fist to release the air. Place on a floured surface and knead until the dough is back to its original size. Roll into a long cylinder and cut into 24 round, even pieces.
  2. Gently flatten each piece into a circle. Use your dominant hand to push with the rolling pin, and rotate the dough with your other hand, flattening only the edges. Keep the center thick to prevent leaks. Your wrapper should be about 5 inches in diameter. Refer to the video above for visual guidance.
  3. Place a few spoonfuls of filling in the center of the wrapper. Hold the wrapper with your non-dominant hand and pinch the edges together with your thumb and index finger of your dominant hand. Refer to the video above for visual guidance.
  4. Let the buns sit for 20 – 30 minutes until puffy.
  5. Boil water in a steamer. After water is boiling, line the steamer with parchment paper and place bao in. Steam for 18 minutes. Remove and serve immediately.

Recipe #3: Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 24 cookies
Ingredients Quantity
All-purpose flour 2 1/4 cups (280g)
Baking soda 1 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Unsalted butter 1 cup (225g), softened
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup (150g)
Brown sugar 3/4 cup (150g), packed
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Eggs 2 large
Chocolate chips 2 cups (340g)

Step 1: Prepare the dough

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Beat in the eggs one at a time until fully incorporated.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
Step 2: Shape and bake the cookies
  1. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  2. Bake for 10 – 12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. The centers should still be soft.
  3. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
